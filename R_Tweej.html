The Visitors’ Center makes a bold architectural statement that uses local natural building materials. Its simple yet appealing floor plan allows for the program’s interpretive requirements while creating a semi outdoor atmosphere that belongs more to the surrounding nature. The building is composed of a large corrugated metal roof over a wooden truss like structure supported by thick stone walls. A second roof made out of modular palm midrib panels and wood beams covers the exhibition spaces. The double roof system allows for the free motion of air dissipating the heat.
